# Money Plant Knowledge Base

## Plant Identity

* Common Name: Money Plant
* Scientific Name: Epipremnum aureum
* Family: Araceae
* Genus: Epipremnum
* Species: aureum
* Plant Type: Ornamental Foliage Plant
* Growth Habit: Climbing Vine
* Life Cycle: Perennial

## Native Region

Native to:

* French Polynesia

Naturalized in:

* India
* Southeast Asia
* Tropical regions worldwide

## Growth Characteristics

* Fast growing vine
* Produces aerial roots at nodes
* Can grow in water or soil
* Suitable for indoor and outdoor shade

## Propagation Knowledge

### Propagation Methods

* Stem Cutting
* Water Propagation
* Soil Propagation

### Cutting Requirements

A successful cutting must contain:

* At least one node
* Preferably one aerial root
* Healthy stem tissue

### Rooting Time

Water Propagation:

* 7–21 days

Soil Propagation:

* 10–30 days

### Best Season

* Spring
* Summer
* Early Monsoon

## Root Development Knowledge

### Early Root Formation Signs

* White root nubs appear
* New leaf emerges
* Stem remains firm

### Advanced Root Development Signs

* Multiple white roots
* Root length > 3 cm
* New growth visible

### Rooting Failure Signs

* Black stem
* Mushy stem
* Yellow leaves
* Foul smell
* Node softening

## Environmental Requirements

### Temperature

Ideal:
18–30°C

Warning:
<12°C or >38°C

### Humidity

Ideal:
50–80%

Warning:
<35%

### Light

Ideal:
Bright indirect light

Avoid:

* Harsh afternoon sun
* Deep darkness

## Water Requirements

### Soil Moisture

Ideal:
40–60%

Low:
<25%

High:

> 75%

### Watering

* Allow top layer to dry slightly
* Avoid constant saturation

## Soil Requirements

### Soil Type

* Well-draining
* Organic rich

### pH

Ideal:
6.0–7.0

## Common Diseases

### Root Rot

Causes:

* Overwatering
* Poor drainage

Symptoms:

* Black roots
* Mushy stem
* Foul odor

### Leaf Spot

Symptoms:

* Brown spots
* Yellow halo

### Bacterial Blight

Symptoms:

* Water-soaked lesions
* Rapid yellowing

## Common Pests

* Mealybugs
* Spider mites
* Scale insects
