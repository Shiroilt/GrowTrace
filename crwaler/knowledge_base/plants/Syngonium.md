# Syngonium Knowledge Base

## Plant Identity

* Common Name: Syngonium
* Other Name: Arrowhead Plant
* Scientific Name: Syngonium podophyllum
* Family: Araceae
* Genus: Syngonium
* Species: podophyllum
* Plant Type: Tropical Ornamental Foliage Plant
* Growth Habit: Climbing Vine / Trailing Plant
* Life Cycle: Perennial

## Toxicity Warning

Contains calcium oxalate crystals.

Can cause:

* Mouth irritation
* Skin irritation
* Pet toxicity

## Native Region

Native to:

* Mexico
* Central America
* Northern South America

Naturalized in:

* India
* Southeast Asia
* Tropical Regions Worldwide

## Growth Characteristics

* Fast-growing vine
* Produces aerial roots
* Juvenile leaves are arrow-shaped
* Mature leaves become lobed
* Easy to propagate

## Propagation Knowledge

### Propagation Methods

* Stem Cuttings
* Water Propagation
* Soil Propagation
* Air Layering

### Best Cutting

Must contain:

* At least one node
* One healthy leaf
* Preferably an aerial root

### Rooting Hormone

Optional

IBA can accelerate rooting.

### Rooting Time

Water:

7–21 days

Soil:

14–35 days

Air Layering:

21–45 days

### Best Season

* Spring
* Summer
* Monsoon

## Root Development Knowledge

### Early Rooting Signs

* White root nubs appear
* Node swelling
* Stem remains firm

### Medium Stage

* Root branching begins
* New leaf growth starts

### Advanced Stage

* Multiple roots >3 cm
* Active vine growth
* New nodes developing

### Failed Rooting Signs

* Black node
* Mushy stem
* Foul smell
* No roots after 45 days

## Expert Notes

Syngonium is one of the easiest tropical plants to propagate.

Cuttings with aerial roots root significantly faster than cuttings without aerial roots.

Node health is the single most important factor in propagation success.

Consistent moisture produces better rooting results than frequent heavy watering.
