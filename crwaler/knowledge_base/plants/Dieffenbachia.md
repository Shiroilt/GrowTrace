## Expert Notes

Dieffenbachia stores water inside its cane-like stem, allowing cuttings to survive for some time before rooting.

Stem firmness is one of the most important indicators of successful rooting.

Long-term waterlogging is the most common cause of cutting failure.

New shoot emergence is often a stronger indicator of rooting success than leaf retention.
