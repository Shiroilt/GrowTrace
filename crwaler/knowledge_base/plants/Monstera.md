# Monstera Knowledge Base

## Plant Identity

* Common Name: Monstera
* Scientific Name: Monstera deliciosa
* Family: Araceae
* Genus: Monstera
* Species: deliciosa
* Plant Type: Ornamental Tropical Foliage Plant
* Growth Habit: Climbing Hemiepiphyte
* Life Cycle: Perennial

## Native Region

Native to:

* Southern Mexico
* Panama
* Central America

Naturalized in:

* India
* Southeast Asia
* Tropical regions worldwide

## Growth Characteristics

* Produces large fenestrated leaves
* Uses aerial roots for climbing
* Fast growth under high humidity
* Can be grown indoors and outdoors

## Propagation Knowledge

### Propagation Methods

* Node Cutting
* Water Propagation
* Soil Propagation
* Air Layering

### Critical Requirement

A cutting MUST contain:

* At least one node

Without a node:

* Rooting impossible
* Survival impossible

### Best Cutting

Contains:

* One healthy node
* One aerial root
* One mature leaf

### Rooting Time

Water:

14–45 days

Soil:

21–60 days

Air Layering:

30–60 days

### Best Season

* Spring
* Summer
* Monsoon

## Root Development Knowledge

### Rooting Indicators

Early:

* White root tip visible
* Node swelling

Middle Stage:

* New root branching
* New leaf begins forming

Advanced:

* New leaf unfurling
* Multiple roots >5 cm

### Failed Rooting Indicators

* Node turns black
* Stem becomes mushy
* Leaf yellows rapidly
* No root activity after 60 days

## Expert Notes

A Monstera cutting without a node will never root regardless of time, humidity, or water quality.

Cuttings with existing aerial roots root significantly faster than cuttings without aerial roots.

New leaf production is one of the strongest indicators that root development has begun successfully.
